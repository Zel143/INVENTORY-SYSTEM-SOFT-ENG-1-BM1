## 2. Executive Summary

**SafeData QC** is developing a secure, centralized B2B Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform designed to document, track, and analyze incidences of abuse and welfare crises at the local government level. 

By uniting data from the Social Services and Development Department (SSDD), drop-in centers, and local NGOs, our system transforms fragmented case processing into a streamlined, data-driven referral network to protect vulnerable populations.

### Key Highlights:
- **Platform Type:** Cloud-based, mobile-responsive web application
- **Target Market:** Quezon City LGU (SSDD) and 800+ accredited NGOs (Department of the Interior and Local Government [DILG], 2023)
- **Revenue Model:** B2G/B2B SaaS subscriptions
- **Core Value:** First unified abuse case tracking system in Quezon City

## 4. Problem Statement

### The Core Issue
Currently, there is an **inadequate system for documenting incidences of abuse** at the local level in Quezon City (Quezon City Social Services and Development Department [SSDD], 2024).

### The Gap We Fill
There is no established database to track abuse against highly vulnerable groups, including:

- 👶 **Children and Youth**
- 👩 **Women**
- 👴 **Elderly**
- ♿ **Differently-abled persons**
- 🌏 **Families of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs)**

Because of this data gap, various welfare agencies **lack a firm basis for determining their target clientele** and appropriately allocating resources.

### Compounding Factors

1. **Temporary Processing Centers:** The city relies heavily on a temporary drop-in center to process cases before referring them to established welfare institutions, making case-tracking highly disjointed (Quezon City SSDD, 2024).

2. **Overwhelmed Staff:** The SSDD is experiencing an increased number of clients in crisis situations, putting additional strain on city resources (Quezon City SSDD, 2024).

3. **No Unified Records:** Cases are documented separately by different agencies, leading to redundant paperwork and lost information during client transfers.

### 🚨 Module 3: Crisis Assistance & Resource Triage System

**The Problem It Solves:** The SSDD is experiencing increased strain from a growing number of clients in crisis situations who lack adequate income and are forced to seek emergency government assistance for daily survival needs like **medicines, transportation, and funeral services**.

**Our Solution:** A standardized triage and flagging system that helps social workers quickly evaluate, verify, and process emergency requests—optimizing the distribution of stretched city resources.

**Crisis Request Categories:**

| Category | Priority Level | Typical Processing Time |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|
| 💊 **Medical Emergency** | 🔴 CRITICAL | Same-day |
| 🚑 **Transportation (Medical)** | 🔴 CRITICAL | Same-day |
| ⚰️ **Funeral Assistance** | 🟠 HIGH | 1-2 days |
| 🍚 **Food/Subsistence** | 🟠 HIGH | 1-2 days |
| 🏠 **Emergency Shelter** | 🟡 MEDIUM | 2-3 days |

### 🔒 Module 5: Security & Privacy Compliance

Given the extremely sensitive nature of abuse victim data, security is non-negotiable:

| Security Feature | Implementation |
|------------------|----------------|
| **Data Encryption** | AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit |
| **Access Control** | Role-based (RBAC) with principle of least privilege |
| **Audit Trail** | Immutable logs of all data access and modifications |
| **2FA Authentication** | Required for all users handling sensitive records |
| **Data Anonymization** | For analytics/reporting (no PII exposed) |
| **RA 10173 Compliance** | Full adherence to Data Privacy Act (Republic Act No. 10173, 2012) |
| **Consent Management** | Digital consent workflows for data subjects |

## Appendix B: Data Privacy Compliance (RA 10173)

SafeData QC is committed to full compliance with the **Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173)** (Republic Act No. 10173, 2012).

### Compliance Measures:

1. **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** - Designated team member responsible for privacy compliance
2. **Privacy Impact Assessment** - Conducted before platform launch

6. **Right to Erasure** - Compliant deletion procedures
